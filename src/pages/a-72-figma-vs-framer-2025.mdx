## The Short Answer

Figma is the design tool. Framer is the build tool. In 2025, the question isn't which is better — it's which one matches what you're trying to do. Figma wins for complex design systems and team collaboration. Framer wins for speed-to-shipping and interactive prototypes. Most professional workflows use both.

---

## What Each Tool Does Best

### Figma: Design at Scale

Figma remains the gold standard for interface design. Its strengths are design systems, component architecture, and real-time collaboration. The tool is built for teams designing complex products at scale.

**Where Figma dominates:**
- Design systems with hundreds of components
- Complex prototyping with real interactions
- Team collaboration and design reviews
- Design-to-development handoff with specs
- Enterprise workflows with permissions and governance

### Framer: Ship Fast

Framer is a design tool that ships. It lets you design and build the same thing simultaneously, which is valuable for landing pages, marketing sites, and simple apps.

**Where Framer dominates:**
- Landing pages that need to ship fast
- Interactive prototypes that feel real
- Content-heavy pages with CMS capabilities
- Design-to-deployment in one tool
- Solo designers who want to build without engineering

---

## Feature Comparison

### Design Capabilities

Figma has deeper design tools. Its component system, variants, auto-layout, and design token management are unmatched. Framer's design tools are improving but still lack the depth for complex products.

**Winner: Figma**

### Prototyping

Both tools do prototyping well. Figma's prototyping is more sophisticated for complex interactions. Framer's prototyping feels more like a real product because it runs in the browser.

**Winner: Tie — Figma for complex, Framer for feel**

### Collaboration

Figma is built for teams. Real-time editing, comments, version history, and team libraries make it the standard for collaborative design. Framer's collaboration tools are functional but not as polished.

**Winner: Figma**

### Build Output

Framer generates production-ready code and publishes to its own hosting. Figma requires plugins or manual handoff to engineering.

**Winner: Framer**

### AI Features

Figma AI generates content, suggests layouts, and automates repetitive tasks. Framer's AI features focus on content generation and code creation.

**Winner: Figma**

---

## When to Use Each

**Use Figma when:**
- You're designing a complex product with many screens
- You're working with a design team
- You need a maintainable design system
- You're handing off to engineering teams
- You need production-ready design specifications

**Use Framer when:**
- You're building a landing page or marketing site
- You want to ship a prototype that feels real
- You're a solo designer who also builds
- You need CMS functionality without coding
- You don't have an engineering team

---

## The Bottom Line

Figma and Framer aren't competitors — they're complements. Figma is where you design. Framer is where you build. The best designers use both. If you can only pick one, pick the one that matches your workflow: Figma for design systems and team collaboration, Framer for speed-to-shipping and solo builders.
