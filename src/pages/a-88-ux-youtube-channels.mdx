## The Short Answer

These 15 YouTube channels offer the best UX design content in 2025. They cover fundamentals, AI tools, career growth, and industry trends. Follow the ones that match your current needs.

---

## Fundamentals

### 1. AJ&Smart

The best channel for UX strategy and design sprints. Practical, no-fluff content on how to run effective design processes.

### 2. Malewicz

Gary Malewicz covers UX design systems, component architecture, and Figma best practices. Excellent for designers who want to deepen their technical skills.

### 3. The Futur

The Futur covers UX design, freelancing, and design business. Great for designers who want to start their own practice or freelance.

### 4. UX Mastery

Chris Standers covers UX fundamentals, research methods, and practical design advice. Clear, actionable content for intermediate designers.

---

## AI and Design

### 5. Figma (Official)

Figma's official channel covers Figma AI, design systems, prototyping, and workflow tutorials. The best resource for Figma mastery.

### 6. Design+Tech

Covers AI design tools, Figma AI, Galileo AI, and emerging AI design platforms. Great for staying current with AI design tools.

### 7. UX AI

Dedicated to AI in UX design. Covers AI tools, agent design, and human-AI interaction patterns.

---

## Career and Industry

### 8. Colin Neblett

Career-focused UX content. Covers interviews, portfolio building, salary negotiation, and career transitions.

### 9. Luke Wroblewski

Luke W covers product strategy, UX trends, and the future of design. Thoughtful, strategic content for senior designers.

### 10. Jesse James Garrett

Covers UX strategy, product thinking, and the evolution of the design field. Deep, reflective content for experienced designers.

---

## Bonus Channels

### 11. Nielsen Norman Group

Academic UX research content. Covers user research, usability testing, and evidence-based design. Excellent for research-focused designers.

### 12. Interaction Design Foundation

Covers UX design education, courses, and industry insights. Great for designers who want structured learning.

### 13. UX Collective

UX design community channel. Covers trends, tools, and industry news. Good for staying informed.

### 14. Design Tutorials for Web Designers

Covers design tools, UI design, and web design. Practical tutorials for designers who build.

### 15. Flux Academy

Covers UX design, product design, and freelancing. Practical content for designers starting out or transitioning.

---

## The Bottom Line

Follow channels that match your current needs: fundamentals for learning, AI channels for staying current, and career channels for growth. Don't follow them all — pick 3-4 that match your goals and follow them consistently.
