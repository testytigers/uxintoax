## The Short Answer

The most demanded UX skills in 2025 are Figma, user research, and AI tools proficiency. AI-related skills appeared in 42% of UX job postings — up from 8% in 2023. Prompt design, conversation design, and AI product design are the fastest-growing skills. Traditional skills like prototyping and design systems remain essential but are no longer sufficient on their own.

---

## Methodology

Here's how we built this analysis:

**Data source:** 1,247 UX job postings from LinkedIn, Wellfound, company career pages, and our own job board collected between January and June 2025.

**Filtering:** We excluded non-UX roles (product management, engineering, data science) and focused on titles containing: UX Designer, Product Designer, UX Researcher, UX Writer, Interaction Designer, Design Systems Designer, and AI/AX Designer.

**Analysis method:** We extracted all skills and tools mentioned in each posting, normalized them (e.g., "AI," "artificial intelligence," and "machine learning" were grouped), and calculated frequency, trend, and correlation by role and company size.

**Limitations:** This analysis reflects hiring *language*, not actual job requirements. Companies may use different terminology for similar skills. Salary data comes from Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, and Payscale cross-referenced with job postings.

## The Top 10 Most Demanded UX Skills in 2025

Based on frequency across 1,247 job postings:

### 1. Figma — 82% of postings

Figma remains the undisputed #1 tool in UX design. It's mentioned in 82% of all UX job postings, up from 78% in 2023. The rise of Figma AI is driving this growth — companies want designers who can leverage Figma's AI features.

**Salary premium:** Designers with "Figma" in their profile earn 12% more on average than those without.

### 2. User Research — 71% of postings

User research remains the second most demanded skill. Despite AI tools for research synthesis, companies still value the strategic thinking behind research design, participant recruitment, and qualitative analysis.

**Trend:** Stable (slight increase from 68% in 2023).

### 3. AI Tools Proficiency — 42% of postings

The third most demanded skill — and the fastest growing. "AI tools," "AI proficiency," or "experience with AI products" appeared in 42% of postings. This is up from 8% in 2023.

**Trend:** Rapidly rising (+34 percentage points in 2 years).

### 4. Design Systems — 38% of postings

Design systems experience is required by over a third of employers. With the rise of AI-generated components and design tokens, design system skills are becoming more technical and more valuable.

**Trend:** Rising (+5 percentage points from 2023).

### 5. Prototyping — 35% of postings

Prototyping skills are still important but their importance is declining relative to AI alternatives. Companies expect designers to prototype quickly, and AI is making that easier.

**Trend:** Slight decline (-3 percentage points from 2023).

### 6. Usability Testing — 33% of postings

Usability testing remains essential. AI tools for testing (Maze, UserTesting) are automating the execution, but the strategic design of tests remains a human skill.

**Trend:** Stable.

### 7. Information Architecture — 28% of postings

IA is undervalued but essential. Companies building complex products — especially AI-powered ones — desperately need designers who can organize information effectively.

**Trend:** Rising (+4 percentage points from 2023, driven by AI products with complex information flows).

### 8. Interaction Design — 27% of postings

Interaction design fundamentals remain core to UX roles. The principles are timeless, but the applications are expanding to include gesture-based, voice, and agent interactions.

**Trend:** Stable.

### 9. AI Product Design — 22% of postings

"AI Product Design" or "AI Product Designer" is becoming a distinct role. Companies want designers who understand how to design for AI systems, not just for static interfaces.

**Trend:** Rapidly rising (up from 3% in 2023).

### 10. Stakeholder Management — 21% of postings

The most surprising entry in the top 10: soft skills. Companies want designers who can communicate with stakeholders, present research, and drive design decisions. This skill only gets more valuable as AI handles more execution work.

**Trend:** Rising (+6 percentage points from 2023).

## Skills by Role Type

### UX Designer (n=512)

Top 5: Figma (85%), User Research (73%), Prototyping (38%), Design Systems (41%), AI Tools (45%)

**Most valuable addition:** AI Tools proficiency (+22% salary premium for those who list it).

### UX Researcher (n=287)

Top 5: User Research (92%), Data Analysis (48%), Survey Design (42%), AI Research Tools (28%), Statistical Analysis (25%)

**Most valuable addition:** Data Analysis (+18% salary premium). AI Research Tools are the fastest-growing research skill.

### UX Writer (n=156)

Top 5: UX Writing (88%), AI Writing Tools (42%), Content Strategy (35%), Tone Guidelines (28%), Accessibility Writing (22%)

**Most valuable addition:** AI Writing Tools (+15% salary premium). UX writers who can use AI tools are producing 3x more content.

### Product Designer (n=292)

Top 5: Figma (79%), Product Strategy (52%), Design Systems (44%), AI Product Design (28%), Stakeholder Management (38%)

**Most valuable addition:** Product Strategy (+20% salary premium). Product Designers with AI skills command the highest salaries in the dataset.

## Skills by Company Size

### Startups (n=412)

Startups demand breadth over depth. They want designers who can do research, design, prototype, and test. AI tools are especially valued because startups need to move fast.

**Top startup skills:** Figma (88%), AI Tools (52%), Prototyping (42%), User Research (68%), Design Systems (32%)

### Mid-size Companies (n=398)

Mid-size companies are transitioning to AI workflows. They're hiring designers who can both maintain existing systems and build new AI-powered features.

**Top mid-size skills:** Figma (84%), Design Systems (45%), AI Tools (48%), User Research (72%), Usability Testing (35%)

### Enterprise (n=437)

Enterprise companies want stability plus innovation. They need designers who can maintain complex design systems while also exploring AI opportunities.

**Top enterprise skills:** Figma (78%), Design Systems (52%), Accessibility (38%), AI Tools (35%), Stakeholder Management (32%)

## What This Means for Your UX Career

### If you're learning something new, learn these 3:

1. **Prompt Design** — The fastest-growing skill with the highest job market demand
2. **Data Analysis** — Becoming essential for evidence-based design decisions
3. **Conversation Design** — The core skill for designing AI agents and interfaces

### If you're deciding what to stop learning:

1. **Adobe XD** — It's dead
2. **Sketch** — Declining rapidly, Figma dominates
3. **Manual wireframing** — Being automated; focus on AI-assisted wireframing

### The salary opportunity:

Designers who combine traditional UX skills with AI skills earn **15-25% more** than those with only traditional skills. The gap is widest for Senior designers (+25%) and narrowing for Junior designers (+12%), because junior designers are entering the market with AI skills already.
