## TL;DR — The Quick Answer

Maze is better for designers who want fast, AI-powered usability testing with built-in participant recruitment and automated analysis. UserTesting is better for teams who need diverse participant demographics, unmoderated sessions with screen recording, and deeper qualitative insights. Choose Maze for speed and AI features. Choose UserTesting for participant quality and qualitative depth.

---

## What is Maze?

Maze is an AI-powered usability testing platform that helps designers run, analyze, and share usability tests. It supports moderated and unmoderated testing, has built-in AI for analyzing results, identifies patterns in user behavior, and generates actionable insights. Maze integrates with Figma, allowing you to test designs directly from your design files.

---

## What is UserTesting?

UserTesting (now part of Forrester) is a platform that connects you with real people who record their screens and voices as they interact with your product. It focuses on unmoderated user testing with screen and camera recording, providing rich qualitative data about how real users interact with your designs. It includes a large participant pool and AI-powered analysis features.

---

## Side-by-Side Comparison

### AI Analysis

**Maze:** Strong AI features. Automatically identifies patterns, generates insight reports, and surfaces recurring issues. The AI analysis is integrated into the testing workflow and produces structured reports.

**UserTesting:** AI features focus on summarizing video recordings and identifying key moments. The AI is less integrated into the analysis workflow and more focused on video transcription and summarization.

**Winner: Maze**

### Participant Recruitment

**Maze:** Built-in participant recruitment with customizable screening criteria. Maze handles the entire recruitment process — finding participants, screening them, scheduling sessions.

**UserTesting:** Large participant pool with diverse demographics. You can filter by age, gender, location, income, and other criteria. However, recruitment is more manual — you set up campaigns and wait for participants to sign up.

**Winner: Maze** (more integrated)

### Qualitative Depth

**Maze:** Good qualitative data from recorded sessions and follow-up questions. The AI helps structure and analyze the qualitative data.

**UserTesting:** Excellent qualitative data with screen recording, webcam recording, and voice recording. The combination of visual, audio, and behavioral data provides richer insights than Maze.

**Winner: UserTesting**

### Ease of Use

**Maze:** Very easy to use. Create a test, design the tasks, recruit participants, and get results — all within the platform. The interface is clean and intuitive.

**UserTesting:** Moderate ease of use. Setting up tests and campaigns requires more steps. The platform is powerful but has a steeper learning curve.

**Winner: Maze**

### Integration

**Maze:** Direct Figma integration — test designs without exporting. Integrates with Slack, Jira, and other design tools.

**UserTesting:** Integrates with most design and development tools. Less direct Figma integration compared to Maze.

**Winner: Maze**

### Pricing

**Maze:** Free tier available. Basic: $35/month. Pro: $99/month. Enterprise: custom pricing.

**UserTesting:** Pay per test. Approximately $50 per participant session. Enterprise plans available with volume discounts.

**Winner: Depends on volume** (Maze is more cost-effective for frequent testing)

## When to Choose Maze

**Choose Maze if:**
- You run usability tests frequently and need an integrated platform
- You want AI-powered analysis that saves hours of manual work
- You need built-in participant recruitment
- You work primarily in Figma and want direct testing integration
- You need fast turnaround on test results
- You want structured insight reports

**Examples:** Weekly usability testing, rapid iteration cycles, Figma-heavy workflows, solo designers or small teams

---

## When to Choose UserTesting

**Choose UserTesting if:**
- You need diverse participant demographics (age, location, income, etc.)
- You want rich qualitative data with screen and webcam recording
- You're running larger-scale studies with many participants
- You need to understand emotional responses (webcam recordings capture this)
- You're a larger organization with testing budget
- You need to share recordings with stakeholders

**Examples:** Annual usability studies, stakeholder presentations, research teams, enterprise organizations

## Our Verdict

For most UX designers and design teams, **Maze is the better choice**. The AI analysis saves real time, the Figma integration is seamless, and the built-in recruitment means you can set up a test in minutes instead of hours. Maze is built for designers who test regularly.

UserTesting is better for organizations that need deep qualitative research with diverse participants. If budget is less of a concern and you prioritize qualitative depth over speed, UserTesting delivers richer insights.

## FAQ

### Can I use both Maze and UserTesting?

Yes. Many teams use Maze for regular weekly testing and UserTesting for deeper quarterly studies. They complement each other well.

### Which platform is better for remote testing?

Both are designed for remote testing. Maze is better for fast, iterative remote testing. UserTesting is better for in-depth remote studies with rich qualitative data.

### Is Maze free?

Maze has a free tier with limited features and usage. For full AI-powered features, you need the Pro plan ($99/month).

### Can UserTesting analyze my tests automatically?

UserTesting has AI features for summarizing recordings and identifying key moments, but it's less comprehensive than Maze's AI analysis. Maze's AI is more integrated into the testing workflow.
