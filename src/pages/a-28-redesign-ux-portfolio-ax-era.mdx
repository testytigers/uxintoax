## The Short Answer

To compete in the AX era, your portfolio needs three things: AI project examples, AI skill descriptions, and evidence of AI workflow. Remove outdated case studies that don't mention AI. Add at least one AI-focused project. Show how AI changed your design process.

---

## What to Remove

### Projects Without AI Relevance

If your case study shows a traditional e-commerce checkout redesign with no AI component, it's not immediately relevant for AX roles. Keep the project — it shows foundational UX skills — but deprioritize it on your portfolio homepage.

### Vague Skill Lists

"I'm proficient in Figma, Sketch, and Adobe XD" is outdated. Replace it with "Figma, Figma AI, Midjourney, Claude, ChatGPT for UX writing."

### Generic Headlines

"UX Designer passionate about user-centered design" is what everyone says. Replace it with "AX Designer specializing in AI product design and conversation design."

---

## What to Add

### AI Project Case Study (1 minimum)

Even if it's a personal project, show at least one AI-focused case study. It should include:
- The problem you were solving
- How AI was part of the solution
- Your role and contributions
- Results and learnings

### AI Skills Section

List your AI skills specifically:
- AI Product Design
- Prompt Design
- Conversation Design
- Agent Experience Design
- AI Research Tools
- AI Prototyping Tools

### AI Workflow Description

In each case study, add a section called "How AI Changed My Process" that describes how you used AI in that project. Did AI help with research? Content? Prototyping? Analysis? Show the process, not just the outcome.

### Interactive AI Demos

If you've built AI-powered prototypes or agents, include an interactive demo. Hiring managers love seeing working prototypes.

## How to Structure Your Portfolio

### Homepage (Above the Fold)
- Headline: "AX Designer | AI Product Design | Conversation Design"
- Subheadline: "Helping companies design better AI products"
- Featured project: Your best AI-focused case study

### Projects Section
- 1 AI-focused project (prominent)
- 2–3 traditional UX projects (still relevant for foundational skills)

### Skills Section
- Core UX skills
- AI-specific skills
- Tools

### About Section
- Your AX thesis
- Why you care about designing for AI
- What you bring to AI product teams

## FAQ

### Do I need a complete portfolio redesign?

No. You don't need to rebuild from scratch. Update your headlines, add one AI case study, and adjust your skills section. That's enough to signal you're AX-ready.

### What if I don't have AI projects yet?

Build one. A personal project that demonstrates AI product design is enough. It doesn't need to be production-ready — just show that you understand the space.

### Should I remove all my traditional UX projects?

No. Traditional UX projects show foundational skills that are still valuable. Keep them, but add AI projects to show you're evolving.

### How important is the portfolio for AX design jobs?

Very. AX design hiring managers want to see practical AI experience. Your portfolio is your strongest proof — more than your resume or LinkedIn profile.
