## The Short Answer

Notion is documentation and knowledge management. Linear is issue tracking and sprint management. UX teams typically use both: Notion for research notes, design rationale, and team documentation, Linear for task tracking, sprints, and design review workflows.

---

## Notion: The Knowledge Hub

### Where Notion Excels
- Documentation and knowledge management
- Research notes and synthesis
- Design rationale and decision logs
- Team wikis and playbooks
- Flexible page structure

### Where Notion Falls Short
- Issue tracking is basic
- Sprint management requires plugins
- Less real-time collaboration on tasks

---

## Linear: The Issue Tracker

### Where Linear Excels
- Fast, focused issue tracking
- Sprint management and planning
- Design review workflows
- Integration with design tools
- Speed and performance

### Where Linear Falls Short
- Limited documentation capabilities
- Not great for knowledge management
- Less flexible than Notion's structure

---

## The AI Factor

Both platforms are integrating AI. Notion AI helps with documentation, content generation, and research synthesis. Linear AI helps with issue prioritization, sprint planning, and workflow automation.

---

## The Bottom Line

Use both. Notion for knowledge. Linear for tasks. They complement each other. AI tools are making both platforms smarter, but they serve different purposes. The teams that use both effectively are the ones that separate documentation from task management.
