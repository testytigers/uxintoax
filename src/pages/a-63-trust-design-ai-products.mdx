## The Short Answer

Trust design for AI products is about designing features that build and maintain user trust in AI systems. The five trust builders are transparency, user control, consistency, honesty, and accountability. The three trust destroyers are inconsistency, overconfidence, and opacity.

---

## The Trust Framework

### 1. Transparency

Show users what the AI is doing and why.

**Techniques:**
- Explain AI reasoning
- Show the steps the AI took
- Reveal AI limitations
- Make AI capabilities visible

### 2. User Control

Give users the ability to override AI decisions.

**Techniques:**
- Easy override mechanisms
- Adjustable AI behavior
- Undo functionality
- Configuration options

### 3. Consistency

Produce reliable, predictable results.

**Techniques:**
- Consistent output quality
- Consistent response format
- Consistent interaction patterns
- Consistent tone and style

### 4. Honesty

Be honest about AI limitations and errors.

**Techniques:**
- Acknowledge uncertainty
- Admit when AI is wrong
- Explain AI limitations
- Provide source citations

### 5. Accountability

Make it clear who is responsible.

**Techniques:**
- Clear disclaimers
- Contact information
- Error reporting channels
- Human review options

---

## Trust Signals

Visual and textual signals that communicate trust:

- Confidence indicators
- Source citations
- Version history
- User reviews and ratings
- Certification badges
- Privacy guarantees

---

## The Bottom Line

Trust in AI products must be designed intentionally. The five trust builders are transparency, user control, consistency, honesty, and accountability. The three trust destroyers are inconsistency, overconfidence, and opacity.

If you're designing an AI product, design trust from the start. Don't treat it as an afterthought.
