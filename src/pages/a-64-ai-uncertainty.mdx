## The Short Answer

Designing for AI uncertainty means designing interfaces that communicate when the AI is confident and when it's uncertain. The techniques are: confidence indicators, alternative suggestions, conditional UI, and adjustable behavior. Confidence scores should be shown when users need to make decisions, not in casual interactions.

---

## The Core Challenge

AI is probabilistic — it doesn't always know the right answer. Users need to understand when the AI is confident and when it's guessing. Designing for uncertainty means making the AI's confidence level visible and actionable.

---

## Techniques for Designing Uncertainty

### 1. Confidence Indicators

Visual signals that communicate AI certainty.

**Types:**
- Numerical (85% confident)
- Categorical (High, Medium, Low)
- Visual (color, opacity, size)
- Textual ("I'm pretty sure..." vs "I know that...")

### 2. Alternative Suggestions

When the AI is uncertain, offer alternatives.

**Techniques:**
- Multiple suggestions with confidence labels
- "Other options" links
- "Not what you wanted? Try..." prompts

### 3. Conditional UI

Change the interface based on AI confidence level.

**Examples:**
- High confidence: Show result directly
- Medium confidence: Show result with a disclaimer
- Low confidence: Show alternatives or ask for more information

### 4. Adjustable Behavior

Let users control AI confidence levels.

**Options:**
- Conservative mode (more certain, fewer options)
- Balanced mode (default)
- Creative mode (more options, more uncertain)

---

## The Bottom Line

AI uncertainty is a fundamental characteristic that must be designed for. The four techniques are confidence indicators, alternative suggestions, conditional UI, and adjustable behavior. Show confidence when it matters, hide it when it doesn't.
