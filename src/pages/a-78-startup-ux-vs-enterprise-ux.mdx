## The Short Answer

Startup UX gives you breadth, speed, and ownership. Enterprise UX gives you depth, process, and scale. AI tools help both — startups use AI to move faster, enterprises use AI to manage complexity. The best career path combines both.

---

## Startup UX: Move Fast, Learn Fast

### What You Gain
- **Ownership** — Your decisions directly impact the product and business
- **Breadth** — You touch research, design, strategy, and sometimes coding
- **Speed** — Decisions happen in days, not months
- **Learning** — You solve problems you'd never see in enterprise

### What You Lose
- **Mentorship** — You might be the only designer
- **Process** — Limited formal UX processes
- **Scale** — Smaller user base means smaller impact
- **Compensation** — Lower base pay (but equity potential)

---

## Enterprise UX: Scale, Process, Impact

### What You Gain
- **Scale** — Your designs affect millions of users
- **Mentorship** — Senior designers and design leaders
- **Process** — Formal research, design, and validation processes
- **Compensation** — Higher pay, benefits, and stock options

### What You Lose
- **Autonomy** — Many stakeholders and layers of approval
- **Speed** — Decisions take weeks or months
- **Variety** — You focus on one product or team
- **Experimentation** — Risk aversion limits bold experiments

---

## The AI Factor

AI tools affect startups and enterprises differently.

**Startups** use AI to compensate for lack of resources. AI handles research, content, and prototyping that would require dedicated specialists. One designer can deliver enterprise-quality work.

**Enterprises** use AI to manage complexity. AI helps with design system maintenance, content generation at scale, and research synthesis across large datasets. AI makes it possible to maintain quality at scale.

---

## The Bottom Line

Do startups first, then enterprises. The breadth you gain as a startup designer makes you more effective in enterprise roles. The process and scale you learn in enterprises make you more effective as a startup designer later. The AI era accelerates both paths.
