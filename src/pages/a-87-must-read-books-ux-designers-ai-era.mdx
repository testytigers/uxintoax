## The Short Answer

The best UX books for AI-era designers cover three areas: UX fundamentals (design thinking, research, interaction), AI strategy (how AI transforms products), and human-AI interaction (trust, explanation, and collaboration). Here are the 10 books that matter most.

---

## UX Fundamentals

### 1. Don't Make Me Think by Steve Krug

The bible of web usability. Still relevant because the principles of clarity, simplicity, and reducing cognitive load apply to AI interfaces even more than traditional interfaces.

**Read for:** Understanding why simplicity matters more in AI interfaces

### 2. Don't Let Go Me: Understanding the Psychology of Trust by Sarah J. Watson

The essential book on trust in digital products. Covers trust patterns, transparency, and explainability — the three pillars of AI UX design.

**Read for:** Trust design, explainability patterns, transparency strategies

### 3. Continuous Discovery Habits by Teresa Torres

The best book on product discovery methodology. Covers opportunity solution trees, continuous discovery interviews, and evidence-based product development.

**Read for:** Product discovery methodology, continuous research

### 4. Just Enough Research by Erika Hall

A practical guide to UX research methods. Covers when to research, what to research, and how to use research results in design decisions.

**Read for:** Research methodology, evidence-based design

---

## AI and Design

### 5. Designing for Trust in AI by Sarah J. Watson

The definitive book on trust design for AI products. Covers transparency, explainability, confidence indicators, and error recovery patterns.

**Read for:** Trust patterns, explainability design, confidence indicators

### 6. Artificial Intelligence in Design by Chris Osborn

Covers how AI is transforming the design process. Includes AI tools, AI-assisted design, and the future of AI in design practice.

**Read for:** AI design tools, AI-assisted workflow, design future

### 7. The Age of AI: And Our Human Future by Henry Kissinger, Eric Schmidt, and Daniel Huttenlocher

Not a UX book, but essential for understanding the broader context of AI's impact on society, work, and human behavior.

**Read for:** Understanding AI's broader impact, future trends

### 8. Human + Machine: Predicting the Work Future by Paul Romain and Chris Schroeder

Covers how human-AI collaboration works in practice. Includes case studies, implementation strategies, and organizational considerations.

**Read for:** Human-AI collaboration patterns, implementation strategies

---

## The Bottom Line

Read the UX fundamentals first, then the AI-specific books. The fundamentals give you the foundation. The AI books give you the context. Together, they prepare you for the next decade of design work.
