## The Short Answer

65% of UX roles in 2025 offer remote or hybrid options. Remote UX jobs pay $125K median in the US, vs $135K for on-site. However, when adjusted for cost of living, remote UX jobs often pay more in real terms. The trend is clearly toward hybrid — pure on-site is the minority.

---

## Remote vs On-Site: Key Data

### Salary Comparison

| Mode | Median | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Remote (US) | $125K | $95K-$160K |
| On-site (US) | $135K | $100K-$175K |
| Hybrid (US) | $130K | $100K-$165K |

### Job Availability

## Cost of Living Adjustment

Remote UX salaries are location-independent, which means they go further in most locations:

| Location | Remote Salary | COL Index | Real Value |
|---|---|---|---|
| SF (on-site) | $135K | 160 | $84K equivalent |
| NYC (on-site) | $140K | 140 | $100K equivalent |
| Remote | $125K | 100 | $125K equivalent |
| Austin | $110K | 95 | $116K equivalent |
| Berlin | $75K | 85 | $88K equivalent |

**Key finding:** A remote UX salary of $125K has more purchasing power than an on-site salary in almost any major city except low COL areas.

---

## The Bottom Line

Remote UX work is the norm, not the exception. 65% of UX roles offer remote or hybrid options. Remote salaries may be slightly lower on paper, but when adjusted for cost of living, they often pay more in real terms. The trend is clearly toward hybrid flexibility, not pure on-site.

For UX designers: remote work is not just an option — it's the dominant model. Don't limit your job search to your local area.
