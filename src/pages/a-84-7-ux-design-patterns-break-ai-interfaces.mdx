## The Short Answer

Seven common UX patterns break in AI interfaces because they assume predictable, deterministic interactions. AI systems are probabilistic, non-linear, and conversational. Here's what breaks and what to do instead.

---

## 1. Linear Navigation

Traditional navigation assumes users move through predictable paths — Home → Settings → Profile → Edit. AI interfaces are non-linear and conversational.

**What breaks:** Users can't navigate to what they need because AI doesn't follow menu structures.

**What to do instead:** Design for natural language input. Let users describe what they need, not navigate to it.

---

## 2. Icon-Only Navigation

Icon-only navigation relies on icon recognition and mental mapping. In AI interfaces, users don't recognize icons — they express intent in natural language.

**What breaks:** Users don't know what the icons represent because they're not looking at menus.

**What to do instead:** Design for text input and conversational context. Icons have a role in AI interfaces, but they're secondary to text.

---

## 3. Breadcrumb Trails

Breadcrumbs show users where they are in a linear path. AI interfaces don't have linear paths — they're conversation flows that branch and loop.

**What breaks:** Breadcrumbs don't represent AI conversation flows because conversations aren't linear.

**What to do instead:** Use conversation history instead of breadcrumbs. Show the conversation flow, not a navigation path.

---

## 4. Loading Spinners for Predictable Delays

Loading spinners work when you know how long something will take. AI responses are unpredictable — sometimes fast, sometimes slow.

**What breaks:** Loading spinners create false expectations about response time.

**What to do instead:** Use streaming responses and progressive disclosure. Show the AI thinking process or partial results as they become available.

---

## 5. Binary Error States

Traditional error states are binary: success or failure. AI systems are probabilistic — they can be partially correct, uncertain, or hallucinating.

**What breaks:** Binary error messages don't capture the nuance of AI errors.

**What to do instead:** Design confidence indicators and explanation interfaces. Show users how confident the AI is and what it's uncertain about.

---

## 6. Pagination

Pagination assumes content is organized into discrete pages. AI generates content dynamically based on user queries, not page structures.

**What breaks:** Pagination doesn't apply to AI-generated content that's continuously created or summarized.

**What to do instead:** Use infinite scroll or conversational continuation. Let users ask for more detail, not navigate to the next page.

---

## 7. Form-Based Input

Traditional forms require users to fill in specific fields. AI interfaces accept natural language input that covers multiple fields implicitly.

**What breaks:** Forms are unnecessary when users can describe what they need in natural language.

**What to do instead:** Use conversational input. Let users describe their needs and have the AI extract the relevant information.

---

## The Bottom Line

These 7 patterns aren't wrong — they're designed for a different kind of product. AI interfaces need different patterns: conversational flows, confidence indicators, natural language input, and probabilistic error handling. The designers who adapt their thinking to AI-first interactions will create the best experiences.
