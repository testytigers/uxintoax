## The Short Answer

A UX designer CV in 2025 is a one-page (or two-page max) impact document that leads with quantified outcomes, includes AI tool proficiency as a distinct skill category, and links directly to your portfolio. It should be clean and minimal — not a design exercise — because its purpose is information retrieval, not visual appeal. AI screening systems parse for keywords, metrics, and standard job titles, so format accordingly.

## The CV Structure That Works

### 1. Header (Top 3 Lines)

```
YOUR NAME
San Francisco, CA | yourname@email.com | linkedin.com/in/yourname
Portfolio: yourname.com | UX Designer
```

**Rules:**
- Name in bold, large font
- One line of contact info
- Portfolio link must be visible without scrolling
- No photo, no age, no marital status (US standards)

### 2. Professional Summary (3-4 Lines)

```
UX Designer with 4 years of experience building products that users love and businesses profit from. Specialized in AI-powered design tools and user research that drives measurable outcomes. Reduced user onboarding friction by 40% at XYZ Company, leading to 25% improvement in 30-day retention.
```

**Rules:**
- State your specialty and years of experience
- Include one quantified achievement
- Mention AI tool expertise if relevant
- No objective statements, no "passionate about" language

### 3. Experience (Reverse Chronological)

```
Senior UX Designer | XYZ Company | Jan 2023 - Present
- Led redesign of core product flow, increasing user retention by 30% within 3 months
- Conducted 40+ user interviews and usability tests, translating findings into 12 design improvements
- Built and maintained design system with 85+ components, reducing design-to-dev handoff time by 50%
- Mentored 2 junior designers and established design critique process adopted company-wide

UX Designer | ABC Startup | Jun 2021 - Dec 2022
- Designed and shipped 3 major product features serving 100,000+ monthly active users
- Reduced user onboarding time from 8 minutes to 3 minutes through research-driven redesign
- Collaborated with engineering to implement responsive design patterns across web and mobile
```

**Rules:**
- 3-5 bullet points per role
- Every bullet has a metric or outcome
- Use strong action verbs (led, designed, built, reduced, increased)
- Focus on business impact, not process
- Quantify everything you can

### 4. Skills (Categorized)

```
Design Tools: Figma, Sketch, Framer, Principle, Maze
Research Methods: User Interviews, Usability Testing, Card Sorting, A/B Testing, Survey Design
Frameworks: Design Thinking, Lean UX, Agile/Scrum, Jobs-to-be-Done
AI Tools: Figma AI, ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney, Relume
```

**Rules:**
- Organize by category
- 5-8 items per category
- Only list tools you use regularly
- Include AI tools as a distinct category
- No proficiency bars or percentage indicators

### 5. Education

```
BA in Graphic Design | University of California, Berkeley | 2018
```

**Rules:**
- Include degree, institution, year
- No GPA (unless you're a new grad with 3.5+)
- No relevant coursework (unless you have no professional experience)

### 6. Optional: Certifications and Awards

```
Google UX Design Certificate | Coursera | 2022
Best UX Case Study | Design Awards Conference | 2023
```

**Rules:**
- Only include if relevant to the role
- Don't clutter — this section should be 2-3 items max

## What to Skip (The Old CV Mistakes)

### Proficiency Bars

"Photoshop: 90%" — This means nothing. Does 90% of what? Design? Photo editing? The metric is meaningless and AI parsers can't read it.

### Objective Statements

"Looking for a challenging role where I can utilize my skills..." — This says nothing about what you've done. Replace with a summary that leads with impact.

### Photos and Personal Information

In the US and most Western markets, photos on CVs are unprofessional and can create bias issues. Skip the photo. Skip the age. Skip the marital status. Skip the hobbies section.

### Long Descriptions of Responsibilities

"Responsible for designing user interfaces, conducting research, and collaborating with stakeholders..." — This lists duties, not achievements. Replace every responsibility with an outcome.

### Outdated Design Elements

Infographic CVs, creative layouts, icon-heavy designs — these look clever but fail with AI parsing systems and ATS platforms. Your CV is a document, not a design portfolio. Save the creativity for your portfolio.

## How to Tailor Your CV for Each Application

The biggest mistake UX designers make is sending the same CV to every application. Tailoring is not optional.

### The 15-Minute Tailoring Process

1. **Read the job description.** Highlight key requirements, tools, and skills.
2. **Match your experience.** For each requirement, find the bullet point in your CV that addresses it.
3. **Rewrite 3-5 bullet points.** Use the exact keywords from the job description. If they want "design system experience," make sure "design system" appears in your CV.
4. **Adjust your summary.** If the role is AI-focused, lead with your AI expertise. If it's research-focused, lead with your research track record.
5. **Check ATS compatibility.** Ensure your CV uses standard headings, no columns, no graphics.

**The tailoring ROI:** A tailored CV gets 3x more interview responses than a generic one. The 15 minutes you save by not tailoring cost you 10x in missed opportunities.

## The AI Screening System Reality

75% of companies use some form of AI or ATS to screen CVs. Here's how to pass them:

### What AI Parses

- Job titles (must be standard: "UX Designer," not "Design Wizard")
- Skills and tools (keywords in skills section)
- Years of experience (calculated from work history dates)
- Education (degrees and institutions)
- Metrics and numbers (quantified achievements rank higher)
- Company names (known companies boost your score)

### What AI Misses

- Design quality (it can't evaluate your portfolio)
- Communication skills (unless mentioned in bullets)
- Cultural fit (assessed in interviews)
- Creativity (assessed in interviews and portfolio)

### The AI-Optimized CV Formula

```
Standard job title + Quantified impact + AI-relevant keywords +
Structured skills section + Clean formatting = High ATS score
```

## CV vs Portfolio: What Each Does

Your CV and portfolio serve different purposes:

**CV:** Gets you the interview. It's a scannable document optimized for AI parsing and human scanning. It leads with metrics and outcomes.

**Portfolio:** Gets you the job. It's a deep dive into your process, thinking, and design decisions. It shows how you solve problems, not just what you achieved.

**The relationship:** Your CV links to your portfolio. Your portfolio supports your CV claims. They work together — one is the headline, the other is the full story.
