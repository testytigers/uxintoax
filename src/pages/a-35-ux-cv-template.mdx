## The Short Answer

Your UX designer CV in 2025 needs to do two things simultaneously: pass through ATS (Applicant Tracking System) filters and impress a human hiring manager. The best CVs use AI to optimize for ATS while maintaining a human voice, include quantified achievements rather than responsibilities, and demonstrate AI fluency in the work experience section — not just as a skills listing.

## The Key Principles

### Principle 1: Quantify Everything

Every bullet point should answer "so what?" with a number.

**Bad:** "Responsible for UX design of mobile app."
**Good:** "Redesigned mobile app onboarding flow, reducing drop-off by 28% and increasing activation by 22%."

### Principle 2: Lead with Impact, Not Process

Hiring managers care about outcomes, not how you got there.

**Bad:** "Used user research, wireframing, and usability testing to design features."
**Good:** "Designed 3 features that increased daily active users by 15% after a 6-week research-to-shipping cycle."

### Principle 3: Demonstrate AI Fluency in Context

Don't just list AI tools. Show how you used them to create value.

**Bad:** "Proficient in ChatGPT and Figma AI."
**Good:** "Used AI-augmented research to process 100+ interview transcripts, reducing synthesis time from 3 weeks to 3 days."

### Principle 4: Keep It to One Page (Unless You're Senior)

Under 10 years of experience? One page. Senior or principal? Two pages max. Every word on your CV should earn its place.

## Optimizing for ATS in 2025

**Keywords to include (if relevant):**
- User experience design / UX design
- Design systems
- User research / UX research
- Prototyping / Wireframing
- Usability testing
- Figma / Figma design
- Design thinking
- Agile / Scrum
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Stakeholder management
- Accessibility / WCAG
- AI-augmented / AI tools / AI workflows

**Format rules:**
- Use standard section headers: Experience, Education, Skills
- No tables, text boxes, or graphics in the CV
- Save as PDF (most ATS handle PDF well now)
- Use standard fonts only
- Include a plain text version if submitting to older ATS

---

## What to Leave Off

- Your photo (ATS can't read it; some companies have anti-bias policies)
- References available upon request (wastes space)
- High school education (unless you're a recent graduate)
- "Soft skills" listed without context (leadership, communication — show them through achievements)
- Generic buzzwords without metrics ("passionate about design," "team player")

---

## The Portfolio Link Matters More Than Anything

Your CV's primary job is to get someone to click your portfolio link. Make it:
- Easy to find (top of CV, next to your name)
- Clean and professional (your domain preferred)
- Fast-loading (no 10-second page loads)
- Mobile-friendly (hiring managers check on phones)

Your CV is the trailer. Your portfolio is the movie. The trailer doesn't need to show every scene — just enough to make them want to watch.
