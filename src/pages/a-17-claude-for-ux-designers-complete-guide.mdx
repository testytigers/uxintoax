## What is Claude?

Claude is an AI assistant developed by Anthropic, designed for natural language tasks including writing, analysis, reasoning, and conversation. Unlike general-purpose AI tools, Claude excels at long-form content, nuanced analysis, and handling large documents. It offers a 200,000-token context window — far larger than most competitors — making it ideal for processing extensive research data.

---

## What UX Designers Actually Use It For

We tested Claude across three UX design teams over four weeks. Here's what they actually used it for:

### Research Report Writing (Most Used)

**What it does:** Take research notes, interview transcripts, or raw data and generate structured research reports.

**What we found:** This is Claude's killer feature. Paste a 50-page research document, and Claude produces a well-structured summary with key findings, quotes, themes, and actionable recommendations in seconds. The output quality is significantly better than ChatGPT — more nuanced, less generic, and more structured.

**Time saved:** ~3 hours per research project

### UX Writing and Microcopy

**What it does:** Generate UX copy — button labels, error messages, help text, empty states, tooltips — tailored to your product's tone and audience.

**What we found:** Claude produces cleaner, more natural-sounding copy than ChatGPT. It's better at avoiding robotic phrasing, understanding context, and generating variations that are genuinely different from each other (not just word swaps). For a product with 50+ microcopy items, Claude can generate all of them in minutes.

**Time saved:** ~5 hours per product

### Content Strategy and Documentation

**What it does:** Write content strategies, documentation, style guides, and design system documentation.

**What we found:** Excellent for structured content. Claude handles long documents without losing context, which is critical for documentation work. It can generate a complete content strategy document from a brief, or turn messy notes into polished documentation.

**Time saved:** ~4 hours per documentation project

### Research Synthesis and Thematic Analysis

**What it does:** Analyze qualitative research data, identify themes, surface patterns, and generate insights.

**What we found:** Claude is impressive at thematic analysis. It identifies recurring patterns, groups related findings, and surfaces quotes that support each theme. The analysis is not a replacement for manual coding, but it's a powerful starting point. It catches patterns you might miss because it doesn't have cognitive biases.

**Time saved:** ~6 hours per research study

## Pros and Cons

### Pros

- **Superior writing quality** — outputs sound human, not robotic
- **Massive context window** — 200K tokens means entire research datasets fit in one prompt
- **Better at reasoning** — stronger analysis and recommendation capabilities
- **Cleaner output format** — structured output that's ready to use, not requiring heavy editing
- **Less prone to hallucination** — more reliable than most competitors
- **Better at nuance** — understands subtle context, tone, and cultural differences

### Cons

- **Slower during peak hours** — can be noticeably slower than ChatGPT during busy times
- **Smaller ecosystem** — fewer integrations and third-party tools compared to ChatGPT
- **Limited customizability** — can't fine-tune the model or adjust generation parameters
- **No visual generation** — can't create images or diagrams (unlike DALL-E or GPT-4o)
- **Web browsing limitations** — Claude's browsing capabilities are more limited than ChatGPT's
- **Less community support** — fewer tutorials, templates, and community resources

## Alternatives Worth Considering

### ChatGPT (OpenAI)

ChatGPT is the alternative for general-purpose use. It has a larger ecosystem, more integrations, and multimodal capabilities (image generation, voice). If you need features beyond writing and analysis, ChatGPT is more versatile.

### Google Gemini

Gemini is Google's answer to Claude and ChatGPT. It has a strong 1M-token context window (even larger than Claude's), good writing quality, and tight integration with Google Workspace. If your team uses Google Workspace heavily, Gemini might be the better choice.

### Custom LLM

For teams with technical resources, fine-tuning an open-source model (like Llama 3) on your own data gives you the most control over output quality and brand voice. This requires more setup but offers the best long-term value for organizations.

## FAQ

### How does Claude compare to ChatGPT for UX writing?

Claude produces more natural-sounding, less robotic copy. It's better at understanding context and generating variations that are genuinely different. For UX writing specifically, we prefer Claude. For general tasks, ChatGPT is more versatile.

### What's the maximum document size Claude can process?

Claude Pro supports a 200,000-token context window, which is roughly 150,000 words or about 300 pages of text. This means you can paste entire research studies, multiple interview transcripts, or comprehensive documentation in a single prompt.

### Can Claude generate images or diagrams?

No. Claude is a text-only model. For image generation, use Midjourney or DALL-E. For diagrams, use tools like Excalidraw or Mermaid.

### Is Claude safe for client work and sensitive research data?

Anthropic has strong safety commitments, and Claude is designed to handle sensitive data responsibly. However, always check your client's data processing agreements and avoid pasting highly confidential information into any AI tool.
