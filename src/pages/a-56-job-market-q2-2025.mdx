## The Short Answer

The UX job market in Q2 2025 is stabilizing after the 2022-2023 downturn. Hiring is up 15% compared to Q2 2024, but the growth is concentrated in AI-integrated UX roles. Traditional UX roles are flat or declining. AI UX skills command a 20-40% salary premium. The data clearly shows that AI skills are the biggest differentiator in the UX job market.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Overall Hiring Trends

Q2 2025 saw a 15% increase in UX job postings compared to Q2 2024. However, this overall growth masks significant shifts in what types of roles companies are hiring for.

**By role type:**
- AI UX Designer: +45% (vs Q2 2024)
- Product Designer with AI skills: +30%
- Senior UX Researcher: +10%
- Traditional UX Designer: -5%
- UI Designer: -8%

**By company type:**
- AI startups: +60%
- Established tech companies: +20%
- Non-tech companies: +5%
- Agencies: -10%

---

### 2. Salary Analysis

AI UX roles command a significant salary premium. The data shows that UX designers with AI skills earn 20-40% more than their traditional counterparts.

**By role (US, median):**
- AI UX Design Lead: $215K
- Product Designer (AI skills): $165K
- Senior UX Researcher: $155K
- UX Design Lead: $150K
- Mid-level UX Designer: $115K
- Junior UX Designer: $75K

**AI premium by level:**
- Junior: +20%
- Mid-level: +25%
- Senior: +30%
- Lead: +35%

---

### 3. Geographic Distribution

Remote work continues to dominate the UX job market. 65% of UX roles in Q2 2025 offer remote or hybrid options.

**Top hiring locations:**
- San Francisco Bay Area: 18% of roles
- New York: 12% of roles
- Seattle: 10% of roles
- Austin: 8% of roles
- Remote: 65% of roles

**Salary by location (median):**
- San Francisco: $145K
- New York: $135K
- Seattle: $130K
- Austin: $110K
- Remote: $125K

### 5. Experience Level Demand

The market is shifting toward mid-level and senior roles.

**By level:**
- Junior (0-2 years): 15% of postings
- Mid-level (2-5 years): 35% of postings
- Senior (5-10 years): 30% of postings
- Lead/Principal (10+ years): 20% of postings

**Hiring by company size:**
- Startups (1-50): 25%
- Mid-size (51-500): 40%
- Large (501-5000): 25%
- Enterprise (5000+): 10%

## What the Data Tells Us

### 1. AI Skills Are the Biggest Differentiator

The data is unambiguous: UX designers with AI skills are in higher demand and command higher salaries. The 45% growth in AI UX roles vs. the -5% decline in traditional UX roles is a clear signal.

### 2. The Market Is Recovering, But Selectively

Overall hiring is up, but the growth is concentrated in AI-integrated roles. Companies are hiring fewer generalist UX designers and more specialists.

### 3. Remote Work Is Dominant

65% of UX roles offer remote options. The geographic concentration is decreasing as more companies embrace remote work.

### 4. Mid-Level and Senior Roles Dominate

The market is strongest for experienced designers. Junior roles are declining, which means it's getting harder for new designers to break in.

---

## The Bottom Line

The Q2 2025 UX job market data shows a clear trend: AI skills are the biggest differentiator. Hiring is up overall, but the growth is concentrated in AI-integrated roles. AI UX designers command a 20-40% salary premium. Remote work is dominant, and experienced designers are in higher demand than ever.

For UX designers: learning AI skills is not optional. It's the single most effective way to stay competitive and increase your earning potential.
