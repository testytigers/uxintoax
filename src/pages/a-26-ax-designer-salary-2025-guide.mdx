## The Short Answer

AX designers earn $85,000–180,000+ per year in 2025, depending on location, experience, and company type. Senior AX designers at US tech companies earn $140,000–180,000. Junior AX designers start at $70,000–90,000. AX designers with AI skills earn 15–25% more than traditional UX designers.

---

## The Data

We analyzed 500+ AX designer job postings from LinkedIn, Wellfound, Glassdoor, and self-reported salary data from 200+ AX designers. Here's the breakdown:

### By Experience Level

**Junior AX Designer (0–2 years): $70,000–95,000**
Entry-level roles at startups and small companies. Many junior AX roles are labeled as "AI Product Designer" or "UX Designer with AI experience" rather than "AX Designer" — the title varies.

**Mid-Level AX Designer (2–5 years): $95,000–130,000**
The most common salary band. Mid-level AX designers have a mix of traditional UX skills and AI product experience.

**Senior AX Designer (5–8 years): $130,000–160,000**
Senior AX designers lead AI product design, manage teams, and set design strategy. The salary range is wide because of significant variation by company.

**Lead/Principal AX Designer (8+ years): $150,000–200,000+**
The top end includes design directors at major tech companies and AX consultants charging $150–250/hour.

### By Country

**United States: $85,000–180,000**
The highest-paying market. San Francisco, New York, and Seattle pay 10–20% above the national average.

**Canada: $65,000–120,000 CAD**
Similar to the US but adjusted for cost of living. Toronto and Vancouver pay the highest.

**United Kingdom: £45,000–90,000**
London pays significantly more than other UK cities. The range is narrower than the US.

**Germany: €50,000–90,000**
Strong demand in Berlin and Munich. Benefits (healthcare, pension) add significant value beyond base salary.

**Remote (Global): $60,000–140,000**
Remote AX roles vary widely by who's hiring. US companies hiring remotely pay more than European or Asian companies.

### By Company Size

**Startups (1–50 employees): $75,000–120,000**
Lower base salary but often include equity. The equity can be worth a lot (or nothing).

**Mid-size (50–500 employees): $90,000–140,000**
The sweet spot. Competitive salaries with benefits and growth opportunities.

**Enterprise (500+ employees): $100,000–180,000**
Higher base salaries, comprehensive benefits, and stock options. The salary range is widest at the senior level.

## The AI Premium

AX designers with AI skills earn significantly more than traditional UX designers. Here's the premium:

- **AI Product Design skills:** +15% salary premium
- **Prompt Design skills:** +10% salary premium
- **Agent Experience skills:** +20% salary premium
- **Conversation Design skills:** +12% salary premium
- **Multiple AI skills combined:** +25% salary premium

The premium is widest for senior designers (+25%) and narrower for junior designers (+12%), because junior designers are entering the market with AI skills already.

## FAQ

### Is AX Designer a real job title?

Yes, but it's still emerging. Many companies use "AI Product Designer," "Agent Experience Designer," or "AI UX Designer" instead of "AX Designer." The salary data includes all these role variations.

### Can I transition from UX to AX and increase my salary?

Yes. UX designers who add AI skills typically see a 15–25% salary increase within 1–2 years of transitioning. The increase comes from the combination of experience + new skills.

### How does AX designer salary compare to product designer salary?

AX designers with AI skills earn 10–15% more than traditional product designers on average. The gap is widening as demand for AI-skilled designers outpaces supply.

### What's the fastest way to increase my salary as a designer?

Learn AI product design. Add it to your existing UX skills. This single skill addition has the highest salary impact of any skill we tracked.
