## The Short Answer

The most in-demand UX skills in June 2025 are AI/ML understanding (55% of postings), Figma (85%), user research (70%), design systems (60%), and prototyping (55%). The most rapidly growing skills are AI/ML understanding (+25%), prompt engineering (+20%), and data analysis (+15%). AI skills command the highest salary premiums.

---

## The Top 10 Skills by Demand

### 1. Figma — 85% of Job Postings

Still the #1 tool skill. Figma is the standard design tool, and 85% of UX job postings list it as a requirement.

**Salary impact:** +5% over non-Figma roles

---

### 2. User Research — 70% of Job Postings

User research remains one of the most in-demand skills. Companies want designers who can validate their decisions with real data.

**Salary impact:** +12%

---

### 3. Design Systems — 60% of Job Postings

Design systems knowledge is essential for mid-level and senior roles. Companies need designers who can build and maintain scalable design systems.

**Salary impact:** +18%

---

### 4. AI/ML Understanding — 55% of Job Postings

The fastest-growing skill. AI/ML understanding is now required in 55% of UX job postings, up from 30% in Q2 2024.

**Salary impact:** +30%

---

### 5. Prototyping — 55% of Job Postings

Prototyping skills remain essential. The ability to create interactive prototypes is still a core UX skill.

**Salary impact:** +8%

---

### 6. Prompt Engineering — 30% of Job Postings

The newest entry on the list. Prompt engineering is now required in 30% of UX job postings, up from 10% in Q2 2024.

**Salary impact:** +25%

---

### 7. Data Analysis — 25% of Job Postings

Data analysis skills are increasingly important as UX roles become more data-driven.

**Salary impact:** +20%

---

### 8. Accessibility — 20% of Job Postings

Accessibility is becoming a standard requirement, not a specialty.

**Salary impact:** +15%

---

### 9. Motion Design — 15% of Job Postings

Motion design is a growing specialty, especially for AI product interfaces.

**Salary impact:** +12%

---

### 10. Coding (HTML/CSS) — 10% of Job Postings

Basic coding skills are still valued, though less important than they were 5 years ago.

**Salary impact:** +10%

## The Bottom Line

The data is clear: AI skills are the biggest differentiator in the UX job market. AI/ML understanding is required in 55% of job postings and commands a 30% salary premium. Prompt engineering is the fastest-growing skill, up 200% in just one year.

For UX designers: the skills that matter most are AI/ML understanding, prompt engineering, data analysis, and design systems. Learn these, and you'll be in high demand.
